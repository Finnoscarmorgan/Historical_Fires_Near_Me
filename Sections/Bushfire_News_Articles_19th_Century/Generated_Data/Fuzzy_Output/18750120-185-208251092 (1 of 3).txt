NEWS AND NOTES. Tuesday was the hottest day of the season hitherto. The sun heat was more intense than on Monday, and the air was made thicker and hotter by bush-fires raging all round the horizon. At night. Mount Buninyong was- lit up with bright red fires, and our country corres pondents send in accounts of fires burning in various . places. At Linton the telegraph poles were burnt, and two lines of fire were converging on the township and threatening greater disaster. In the sun at noon, or a little after, the thermometer registered over 130, ’and in the shade over 90.
