([From our Country Correspondents.] WILLIAMS RIVE&, March 29. Strange to say nothing has yet been ascertained of the fate of the poor fellow John Murray, who strayed into the bush some months ago. He was employed in the harvest field and had been indulging very freely in a drinking bout with some companions. Having made up his mind to go to the main line from Perth to Albany, some miles distant, in order to intercept the ' mail cart and ascertain whether there were any letters for him, he set off on foot, and that was the last to be seen of him. There is very little doabt that, becoming exhausted, he lay down and died, and that the dingoes have eaten him.
