BUSH-FIRE AT BUNINYONG. A very extensive fire, but one which happily did not do much damage, occurred on Saturday morning at Mount Buninyong, one-half of which j was swept completely bare by the raging ele- ment, with the exception of the tall trees. The fire is supposed to have originated through the grass being set alight by a farmer named Joseph Innes, while in a drunken state. When the smoke was seen ascending in large volumes, the 1 Buninyong fire-bell was rung, and the firemen hastened with all speed towards the Mount.
