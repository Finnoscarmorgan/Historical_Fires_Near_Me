When the depression became acute Andrade lost everything, and presently getting together what assistance he could, decided to go on the land. He secured a small area adjacent to the Village Settle- ment at Sassafras, off Fern Tree Gully, and has since then been raising his subsistence from the soil—a matter relatively easy for him, as he is a vegetarian—and conducting also a small store, the local post office, etc., up to the other day, when the bushfire swept everything away. 327.
