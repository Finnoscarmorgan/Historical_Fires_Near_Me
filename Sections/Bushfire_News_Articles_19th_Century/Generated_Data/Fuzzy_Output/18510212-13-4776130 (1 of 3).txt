BUSH FIRES. (From the Portland Guardian.) Yesterday forenoon was a period of extra- ordinary heat, and we are sorry to say, of ca- lamity also. The heat from 11 o'clock, am, until afternoon was most oppressive ; a hot wind blowing from the N.N.W. in a most furi- ous manner. At this time the thermometer stood for an hour by one glass at 112° while by two others it reached 116° in the sun. The dust in the streets was most suffo- cating, penetrating the smallest crevices, and filling the houses.
