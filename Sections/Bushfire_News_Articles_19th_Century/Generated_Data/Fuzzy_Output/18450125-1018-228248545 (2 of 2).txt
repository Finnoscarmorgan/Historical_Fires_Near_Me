Bush Fires. — Within the last few days the weather has become exceedingly sultry, the ground parched, and the grass dried up in every direction ; and as the vegetation in the earlier part of the year was exceedingly luxuriant, the quantity of dry combustible material is perhaps greater than in any yeear since the province was colonized. The consequence is, that where bush fires have commenced they have been more than usually destructive. Amongst other casualties, we have heard that at Mr. Austin's station at the Leigh, one of the shepherds was so severely scorched that he is not expected to recover, although he has been brought to Geelong for medical aid. At the same time forty sheep were killep by the flames. It. is reported that Mr. Carfrae of Lake Colac has lost no less than a thousand sheep from the same cause.
