The stoker brought the train to a standstill, and the driver remounted. When about fourteen miles from University Block a slight fire was discovered in the carriage. A spark from the engine had lodged on the outside, and by some means ignited tbe flooring near the door. It was soon extinguished and no further mishap occurred during the journey. The mails were not brought up by the train as expected, and disappointment was expressed in consequence. Bush fires in the neighbourhood have con- . sumed about a thousand acres of grass. About 600 acres of grass and two miles of fencing were burnt on the Wirrega Station yesterday. The blackfellow, Lazy Jimmy, is still at large, but he has been frequently seen near the town. He is a -violent fellow, and it is disgraceful that he should be allowed to defy the law so openly. EOHUNGA, March 22.
