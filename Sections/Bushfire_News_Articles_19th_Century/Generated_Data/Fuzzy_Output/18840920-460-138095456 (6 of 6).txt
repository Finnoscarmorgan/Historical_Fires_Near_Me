The amount of debris—burnt bark, leaves, and grass—that was carried up and to leeward that day by the heat, the smoke, and the gale, must have been immense, as vessels that were 300 miles from the east coast of New South Wales reported some of it to have fallen on their decks. Some time after dark a delightfully cool sea breeze reached our district, which helped materially to extinguish the partially subdued fires,that still continued to bum; but no rain fell in our locality that night, nor the next day.
