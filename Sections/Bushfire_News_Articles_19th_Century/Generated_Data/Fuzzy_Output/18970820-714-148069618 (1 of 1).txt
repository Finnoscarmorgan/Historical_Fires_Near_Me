'Pew fences run directly from one i station homestead to another, but this does not interfere with the working of the telephone. While at Yarrabee Park lately (writes " Bruni") I noticed how well the telephone worked and learned that it ran in a zig-zag fashion for over 20 miles to the next station and was continued for over a dozen miles to another station. Conversation was easily carried on over 35 miles' of wire fencing. The value of such a telephone in locating a bushfire is in valuable. Mr. Stead, the manager of Yarrabee Park, told me that when out on the run in summer he carried a port able telephone, and in case of fire he (knowing the fence used for the tele phone between the homesteads) would connect with the wire and give timely warning. The connection with the i railway-station saves a deal of trouble, ; as inquiries about goods, trucks, &c.;, and the sending away of telegrams can be managed without leaving the house.
