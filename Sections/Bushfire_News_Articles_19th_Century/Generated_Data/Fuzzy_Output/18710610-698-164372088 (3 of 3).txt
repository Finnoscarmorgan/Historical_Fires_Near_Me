The following decision was then given : — ' The Court finds tbat there is no blame to be attacked either to the captain or any one of the mates, bot any of tbe crew on board ; attributing the cause of the stranding of the iron British ship Queen of the Thames, — firstly, to the disarrangement or deviation of the compasses on board ; seoondly, to the unsteadiness and uncertainty of the current in that locality ; thirdly, to a bright fixed light, and a bush-fire that was buraing before the accident, and which was calculated to mislead the managers of that vessel. The Court has therefore no reason for cancelling or suspending the certificates of either the captain or any of the mates, but is of opinion tbat they are respectively entitled to retain the same. — J.
