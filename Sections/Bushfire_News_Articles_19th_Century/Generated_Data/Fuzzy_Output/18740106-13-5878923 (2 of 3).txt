"Not for the last 10 years," writes the Smythesdale correspondent of the Ballarat Star, "has the atmosphere in this locality been so stilling from the smoke of bush fires as it has been sinco,Now Year's Day. Several places in this district have narrowly escaped being burned down. On Friday night Ser- geant Wigmore, assisted by the other police and many of the local residents, had to use the most strenuous exertions in order to prevent the bush fire from burning down the local powder-magazine. During the time the sergeant was thus engaged he fell down one of the numerous shallow holes in the vici- nity, and was very severely hurt, The same night, and also on Saturday, Mrs. Wilson's cottage, in the bush, near Smythesdale, was1 only saved by the exertions of 30 or 40 people, assisted by the police. The flames, which were described as terrific, were within a tew yards of the building.
