Wagga Wagga, 23rd.—323 bullocks and cows, Comongin South to Albury, C. M'Lean, owner. Victoria..—Fine and hot weather has pro railed of late. As regards the grazing prospects, a local paper remarks that the splendid growth of grass, the result of the November rains, created hopes of an abundant supply all through the summer and autumn, but complaints are now general everywhere regarding its rapid disappearance owing to the ravages of the grasshoppers, caterpillars, and bush-fires. Unless a good rainfall occur early in this month landowners will be compelled to oonsiderably lessen the number of their stock. Water, however, is plentiful in all parts of the oolony. The Chief Inspector of Stock has received intimation of a disease having broken out among the cattle in the neighbourhood of Portland, resembling the " rickets," whioh ia believed to arise from weakness of bone.
