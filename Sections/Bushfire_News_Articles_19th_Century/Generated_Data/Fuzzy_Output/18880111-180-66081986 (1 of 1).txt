NOTES FROM YORK. From our own Correspondent. The Christmas and New Year festivities are now over, and we have again settled down to our former quiet state. The weather has been very severe of late, the thermometer at noon on Sunday last registering no less than 108 degrees in the shade. Bush-fires also surrounded the town, and they aided considerably in making the temperature uncomfortable. At the present time strong easterly gales are blowing, raising clouds of dust in the streets, making it very disagreeable indeed. The Municipal Council might, I think, prevent this nuisance by putting on a water cart, which could be done at very little expense. The larrikin element has been rife here during the past few days.
