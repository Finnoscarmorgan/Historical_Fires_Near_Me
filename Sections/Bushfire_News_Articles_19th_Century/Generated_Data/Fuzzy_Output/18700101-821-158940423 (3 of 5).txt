BUSH FIRES. It fortunately seldom falls to our lot, even in this bush-fire-stricken colony, to record such a tragic event as recently occurred on Yorke's Peninsula. Bush fires ordinarily content themselves with a slow and stately march through the country, destroying in their course every blade of under vegetation, and shrivelling the foliage to the summit of the highest trees. In the present day, at all events, tis an unusual thing for beasts to be overtaken by their fiery pursuer, and very rarely indeed do men fall victims to it.
