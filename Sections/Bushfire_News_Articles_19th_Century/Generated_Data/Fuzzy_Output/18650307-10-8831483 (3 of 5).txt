Between 12 and 1 o'clock, the gale seemed to be blowing its hardest, and at that time the roof of Jones's Brewery, McCrae-street, was blown off. The iron roof and verandah of Mr. Savern's dwelling, Bernal street, was also carried away by the wind. One of the front windows of the Mechanics' Institute was blown right in, and fell on a beautiful speci- men of coral in a glass case, both of which were smashed. The bush fires were unprecedented in the memory of the oldest resident. We were informed by a gentleman who arrived from Echuca by the evening train, that the whole of the bush in the neighborhood of Goornong, and indeed on both sides of the line from Runnymede to the latter-named place, was burning fiercely. In the direction of Huntle, and between there and the Whipstick, there were bush fires ; away towards Meyers'-flat immense bodies of smoke and flames were to he seen throughout the day, and again in the direction of Bullock Creek and Lock- wood there were extensive fires.
