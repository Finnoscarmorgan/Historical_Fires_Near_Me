About four o'clock in the afternoon of Monday the town of Goulburn was completely, enveloped in smoke, driven in upon us by a series of cross currents of air. The bush on the ranges round the town was one general blaze for several miles, and that part which appeared gloomy in the distance was smouldering and producing a suffocating smoke. In the neighbourhood of Gunning the fires raged with great fury, and we have been assured by an eye-witness that he travelled thirty-two miles with almost insupportable bush-fires on each side of him. They extended from Mrs. Howell's station, on the Brewer road, to within two miles of Gunning. Instead of approaching Gunning in a direct line, it wheeled off round towards the ranges, and after having devastated in that direction, it approached within two miles of Gunning, when it was stayed. We have been informed that two paddocks of wheat belonging to Mr. Ryan have been totally destroyed.
