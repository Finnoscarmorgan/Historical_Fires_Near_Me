But the bush-fire is another and a totally different soourge ; it is merciless in its might, and when encouraged onwards by the veriest breath of wind, there ie scarcely any power on earth oapable of staying its progress. It leaves in its train nothing but the charred remains of what were once homesteads and the other buildings on every settler's hold- ing. The Kurrajong last week was sub- jected to this terrible ordeal, and many of the best-known residents have suffered very severely from the ravages of bush-fires-so much so that more than one family was left without a roof for the shelter of its members.
