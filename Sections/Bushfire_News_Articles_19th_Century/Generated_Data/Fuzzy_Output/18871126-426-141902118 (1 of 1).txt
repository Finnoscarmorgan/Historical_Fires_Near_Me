PASTORAL INTELLIGENCE. Western Herald OFFICE, FRIDAY. WARM, windy weather has prevailed since our last, and as clouds of dust have been hovering round for the greater part of the time, it can hardly be termed a pleasant state of affairs. The cool nights have, however, been a redeeming feature. None of the pastoral districts report rain, which would, how- ever, be welcomed in very many parts of the colony, if only to lessen the danger of bush-fires. The River Darling continues falling throughout its entire length. Locally it is 1ft 10in above summer level, Louth, 3ft 9in, both falling ; Brewarrina reports summer level. A freshet is reported in the Bogan but it is hardly likely to continue for long or to affect the main stream. The highest temperature during the week has been 102, which was registered on Monday last, the average for the week being 96. Says the Dubbo Dispatchâ€”The weather during the past week has been fine.
