SOUTHERN DISTRICTS (From tho Goulburn Papers of Saturday.) BUSH FIRES.-The inhabitants were gratifying them- selves about the beginning of the week that the sum- mer was breaking up, and that autumn was about set- ting in. However the last two days have served com- pletely to disappoint these amicipations, the weather having been excessively hot. To add to the exhaustive heat of the temperature, Goulburn appears to have been surrounded with desolating bush-fires. A dense mass of smoke appeared above the horizon all day on Thursday and yesterday in the direction of Tarlo. The same appearances were also observable in the direction of Governor's Hill. Although a fire is not so dreadful a calamity in the country as in Sydney, it is productive of considerable injury to farmers. And it is extremely reprehensible in anypart'c, travelling when they do not use every precaution to extinguish the fires they may have occasion to light.
