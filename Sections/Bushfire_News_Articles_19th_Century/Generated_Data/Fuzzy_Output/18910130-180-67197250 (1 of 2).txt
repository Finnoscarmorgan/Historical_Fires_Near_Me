NEWS FROM YORK. THE INQUEST ON THE DEATH OF MICHAEL JOY—THE JURY'S VER- DICT-THE WEATHER— THE TOWN SURROUNDED BY BUSH FIRES. [By Electric Telegraph.] YORK, January 29. The inquest touching the death of Michael Joy, who was found dead by Con- stable Powell at Woonwooning, near Cub- bine, on Thursday last, was held here to- day. The Resident Magistrate (Mr. F. A. Hare) acted as Coroner, while Messrs. Sullivan and Collins formed a jury. The evidence adduced was that of George Mar- tain, in whose employ Joy was at the time of his death, and his wife, Ann Martain, Constable Powell, and the medical officer.
