THE WEATHER AND BUSH-FIRES. Accounts of the damages caused by bush files continue to be received, though the fires themselves have now for the most part been effectually quenched. According to the Hamtiton Spectator, "the Victoria ranges presented a grand sight from Hamilton on Thursday night, the mountains being one mass of flame. On and Wednesday 17 selectors at Victoria Valley were burnt out, and nearly all of them lost their fences and crops, although their houses were saved. On the selections some 4,000 acres of grass were destroyed, and how much more pasturage outside of them it would be difficult to say. Messrs. Field, Matthews, Lang, Fowler, Egan, Callag
