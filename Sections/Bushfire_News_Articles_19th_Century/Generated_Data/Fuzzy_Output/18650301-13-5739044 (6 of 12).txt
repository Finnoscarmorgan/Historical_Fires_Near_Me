It is also rumored that Mr. Govett's station has been devastated by the fire and the home station dwellings, &c.;, completely swept away. There is little doubt we shall hear of very much more destruction yet. A smart shower of rain has fallen, put- ting an end to further danger at present, but the fires are still burning in patches especially amid the trees. NEWSTEAD. Newstead was yesterday surrounded by bush fires and many of the inhabitants are apprehensive for the safety of the town. As a girl was washing in Marsden's yard, sparks from a fire lighted outside flew on to a dry dung heap; the straw immediately ignited and had it not been for the strenuous exer- tions of a score of men, several of the houses would most probably have been consumed. The bush fire on the range near the Bridge Inn was at three o'clock towering yards high.
