GRATFON. Friday. [Evening JVews.]-The lengthened spell of dry weather has broken, and there have been some light showers in the vicinity of the town. Outside of the municipality there was very heavy rain last night. It is reported that the House at Mountain View, the pro- perty of Mr. David Laird, was completely gutted by fire last night. It was only partially insured, mostly in the Victorian Insurance Company. Bush-fires have been prevalent in the vicinity of the house for some days, but they had been quite subdued. The house has boen untenanted for a considerable time, the proprietors having another house where they reside, at some distance from the place de- stroyed. The house, which is built of stone, and the land on which it was built, was ad- vertised as the first prize in the Mountain View Estate Lottery. The ticket holders are anxious as to the results.
