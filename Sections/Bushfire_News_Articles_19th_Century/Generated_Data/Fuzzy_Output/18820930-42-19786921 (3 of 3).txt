At last he disappeared. Leaving my companion to follow him, I returned to the melancholy task of picking up what few things I was likely to find. Just imagine what the task was of trying to find all sorts of articles, from a dinner knife to a compass, in a burnt country, or where the grass was as high and thick an an oat paddock, I shall never forget the miseries of that afternoon; the heat was fearful, with bush-fires all round, my com panion was away, the blacks were all around us, and I had nothing with me to scare them away. My task was to search for the scattered baggage and look after the horses. About nightfall I had recovered about half the things, and got them into a heap where I covered them with bark.
