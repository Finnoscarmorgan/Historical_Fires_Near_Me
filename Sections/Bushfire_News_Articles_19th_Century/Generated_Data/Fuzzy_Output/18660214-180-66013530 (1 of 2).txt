The following is from our own ow respondent :— An extensive bush-fire took place some days since, on a sheep run some miles from the Bay, and but for the timely aid at hand, would have destroyed what little herbage there yet remains. In the immediate vicinity of the town, a vast conflagration took place— caused, it is averred, by the natives. A few hundred yards north of the Convict Depot an immense body of flame came tearing over the hill, driven by a fiercely hot east or land wind.
