We had dismantled the neglected breakfast table, and rearranged it with more regard for compactness than ele gance, ready for the men's supper; and at last the long hot day was nearly over. Having nothing particular to do, I went and eat under the back verandah to rest. Mrs. Jones did likewise, and leaning her elbows on her knees and her chin on her hands, gazed silently up wards at the smoke that told of the fight still going on. Mrs. Brown seized a broom and proceeded to sweep up the leaves scattered about by our discarded decorations, talking meanwhile about other bush fires she had seen. How that the fight was no longer in sight, the sense of excitement and conflict we had felt all day in some degree abated.
