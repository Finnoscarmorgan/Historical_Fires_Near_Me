The husband, was at work,-and the wife and eight or nine children were at home when the fire came down on the house. The whole •of the family at once took to the bush, and the .children were very severely scorched, especially about the feet, through having, to walk on the burning embers. The misery of the sufferers has been intensified by the heavy and continuous rains which have fallen since Tuesday night. No such disaster is believed to have been recorded in the history of the colony in connection with bushfires. The flames spread over a large extent of country,' almost from Camperdown' back to Apollo Bay. It is feared the whole of the losses have not yet been recorded. It is pro posed here to start a public subscription for the relief of those who have been ren dered homeless and destitute by the fires!”
