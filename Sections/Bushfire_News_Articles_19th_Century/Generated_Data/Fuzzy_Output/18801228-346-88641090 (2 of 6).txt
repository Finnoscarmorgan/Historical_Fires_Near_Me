BUSH FIRES. An almost regular accompaniment in our summers are bush fires, and the present season in that respect is no exception to the rule. For the past few days fires have been raging in various parts of the surrounding country, and great damage to property has resulted. A few days ago we recorded two very destructive fires in the Shelbourne district. On Christmas Day dense volumes of smoke were discernible from the city in the neighbor- hood of the Big Hill. The cause of the fire there is unknown, but it found its origin in a paddock of Mr. Seeber, and consumed the fence enclosing the land.
