BOWNA. The peaceful equanimity of this little village was suddenly disturbed last Sunday morning, between 11 and 12 o'clock, when smoke was seen issuing from the direction of Tabletop, which turned out to be an enormous bush-fire in one of Mr. Keogh's paddocks, adjoining Tabletop. Of course all the people living in the vicinity turned out to lend a helping hand (some though, did not think it worth while, or perhaps did not like working on a Sunday), and worked hard for' several hours; at length their efforts were successful, and the fire was beaten down to a great extent, but trees, logs, and stumps are stilly burning, and have to be closely watched.
