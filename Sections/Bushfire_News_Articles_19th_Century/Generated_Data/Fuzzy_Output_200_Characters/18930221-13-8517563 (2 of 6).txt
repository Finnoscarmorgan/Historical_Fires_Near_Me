BUSH FIRES IN THE COUNTRY THE TOWNSHIP OF TARADALE IMPERILLED. TARADALE, SUNDAY. For several days past an immense bush fire has been ranging in the ranges between Taradale and Fryerstown, but as it was in the Government forest reserve it was regarded as likely to be productive of good rather than harm. The strong wind prevailing on Satur- day, however, suddenly chopped round to the north, causing the fire to travel rapidly m the direction of Taradale. The township was soon enveloped in clouds of black smoke, and in the midst of the storm the fire bell was vigorously tolled, business was at once suspended, and hundreds of men left the town to fight the flames.
