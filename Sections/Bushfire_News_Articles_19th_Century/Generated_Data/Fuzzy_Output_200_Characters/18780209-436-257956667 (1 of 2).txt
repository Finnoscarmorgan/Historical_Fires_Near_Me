STOCK AND STATION MEMS. A telegram states that the Western country along the railway route presents a deplorable appearance ; the grass is parched up, the creeks all dry, bush fires are raging in all directions, and the cattle starving. A Penrith telegram states that the country all along the line to Orange presents a parched appearance. Large creeks and water courses are dried up. Byndon Gully and Seventeen-mile Hollow are ravaged by bushfires, Numbers of cattle are dying on all sides. Should the drought continue the farmers will be ruined. A correspondent informs the Forbes Times that the continued hot weather on the Humbug coun try is causing immense losses to stockowners. The heat is something terrible.
