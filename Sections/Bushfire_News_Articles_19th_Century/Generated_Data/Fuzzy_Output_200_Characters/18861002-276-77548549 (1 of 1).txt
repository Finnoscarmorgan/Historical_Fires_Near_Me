The'escort consisted of a dozen well mounted and well-armed policemen, with revolvers in their belts, repeating carbines slnng at their backs, and helmets on their heads. When they reached a suitable spot at night, they encamped and bivouacked in the open air. It was no pleasure trip. At frequent inter vals the emigrants had to traverse swamps of Btioky mud that clung to the wheels of the wagons like glue. They passed through forests savage and dense. Sometimes they saw traces of great bush-fireB that had swept the country with devouring flames," leaving here and there great tracts ol dead, scorched trees, that loomed up in the night against the . starry sky like ghastly skeletons.
