With regard to Bryant & May's lucifers, they are notto be trusted ; indeed ihey are no more entitled to be called a patent safety match than any of the others, being sometimes far more dangerous, I can assure you. Don't, there fore, create a useless monopoly when there is no necessity at all for it. The course indicated above, if made compulsory by law on all travel lers, will speedily check bnsh- fires. Have the roads well patrolled, compel all bushmen to carry a tomahawk or small light pick for cutting : the hole required out, and if they light a fire at all on the surface of the ground, instead of under the surface, duiing the six mouths of October, November, December, January, Feb ruary, and March, let those parties bo infringing the law be liable to imprisonment {without fine), and you will in future have very few bush-fires here. I am, Sir, &c;, A BUSHMAN. Adelaide, December, 1867.
