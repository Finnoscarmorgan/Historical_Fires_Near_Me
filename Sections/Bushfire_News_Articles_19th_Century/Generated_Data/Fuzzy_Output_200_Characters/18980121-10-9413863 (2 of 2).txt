BUSH FIRE AND SAWMILLS. TO THE EDITOR OF THE MERCURY SIR,—I had time, after repairing the damage caused by the late bush fires, to visit Ida Bay and Recherche. At both places there has been terrible destruction of property. Ida Bay, a few weeks ago, was a thriving, busy place; the sawmill had been lately moved from the water's edge into the forest, the mill well cleared round and scrub burnt; men's huts, blacksmith's shop, stables, etc, built, and all the appliances for a well appointed bush mill. The mill was from 2½ to 3 miles from the water, a wood tram the means of outlet. When I visited the place yesterday what a scene of desolation!
