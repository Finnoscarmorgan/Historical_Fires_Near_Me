IN AN INFERNO. BESEIGED BY BUSH FIRES. On February 6th, 1851, the province of Victoria suffered one of the most memorable events of her history. After a succession of extremely hot days, the whole country became en veloped in a sheet of flame. " Farm houses, fences, crops, orchards, hay stacks, bridges, woolsheds were swept away by the impetuous oil-rush of the flames, which left nothing behind them but, a charred heap of ruins and a scene of pitiable desolation.
