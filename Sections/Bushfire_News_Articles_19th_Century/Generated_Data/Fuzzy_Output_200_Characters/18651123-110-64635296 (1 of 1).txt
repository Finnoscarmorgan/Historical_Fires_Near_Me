During the half-hour that the hot wind continued to blow with such fearful violence it occasionally veered round to the westward, and attimes appeared to blow from nearly all points of the compass. The moon shone brightly te whole time, and the south-western horizon was lighted up by sheet lightning. The accounts received from the bush speak of trees uprooted in all direc- tions, fences blown or dashed down, and bush- fires fanned into fury by the wind. The road to the Nine-Mile is rendered in many places impassable, through the number of broken trees lying across it. Great damage has been done to the telegraph wires through- out the entire district. Near the Rev. John K. Macmillan's house, a large tree fell on the wires and broke them. The same thing has happened near the Vine hotel ; also between Beechworth and the Black Springs, and of various other places along the line of tele- graph.
