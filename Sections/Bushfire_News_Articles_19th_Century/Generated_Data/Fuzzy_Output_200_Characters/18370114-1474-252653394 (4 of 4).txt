The fire- sticks which the Aborigines con- stantly carry about with them ; and the embers which they, and white-fellows tra- velling never think of extinguishing, and which indeed it would be in general impos- sible to extinguish, where they have nan- gara (Anglice—spent the night), and even the fire-stick kindled to light the pipe or cigar incessantly smoked by Settlers in the interior;—either of these occasions is alone sufficient to account for the bush-fires so prevalent throughout the summer months. if any remedial measure can be suggested by any Reader of ours, we shall be thankful for the favor of hearing from him.
