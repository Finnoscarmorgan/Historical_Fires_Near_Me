Persons resident on the gold fields destroy also ; an immense number of fine trees, by taking sheets of bark from them for roofing their huts. Instead of at once felling a tree, and removing from it several sheets of bark of the required size, they generally take one sheet of bark only from each tree, which is left standing with a portion of its trunk entirely denuded of bark; and it soon perishes, and ultimately becomes a prey to bush fires.
