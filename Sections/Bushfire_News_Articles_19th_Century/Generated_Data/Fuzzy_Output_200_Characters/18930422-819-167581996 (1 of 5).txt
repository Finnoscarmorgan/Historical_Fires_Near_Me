 saw him moving about the stack and at first 1 thought it was a beast. I saw him come away from the stack. I then identified him. When he left the stack I saw the blaze of the fire and the fire was caused by the stack in question which was alight. There was a fence between the stack and the road. The stack was 2 or 3 yards from the fence. I saw Ward come through the fence on to the road. I was distant about 14 yards from him when he came through the fence. I saw his face and am positive that it was the prisoner. He didn't speak to me. Just after he came through the fence he said " There are plenty of bush fires about". I said " good afternoon." He went in the direction of Bunbury. I went on down the road and told my brother that uncle's straw was alight. My brother James went to my uncle's farm. I told him 1 saw Ward at the stack. There were no fires near there except at the straw stack. There was nothing that could set that stack on fire that I could see. Ward was not smoking. By the Bench.—There was no one else about there at the time. The prisoner made no attempt to extin guish the fire.
