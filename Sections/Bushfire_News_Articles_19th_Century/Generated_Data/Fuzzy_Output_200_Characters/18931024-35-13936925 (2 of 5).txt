It is to have the midsummer menace of Australia brought very close to us when we may see the clouds of smoke from forest fires wafted to north and south of the capital, and read of conflagrations along the Parra- matta and of shipmasters navigating their vessels into port through seas lighted by the flaming headlands. During last week the succession of hot winds prepared the country over which they passed for an outbreak of bush fires, and in the metro- politan counties the fires came, luckily without the widespread damage which in settled regions they are capable of doing. For that we may be grateful, but it would not be amiss to look rather beyond the immediate occasion for gratitude and con- sider the possibilities of the near future. It already seems likely that the season before the country will be found exacting. Though early, the summer begins to be hot and dry.
