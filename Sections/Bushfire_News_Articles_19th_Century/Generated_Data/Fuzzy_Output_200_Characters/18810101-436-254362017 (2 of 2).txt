All the particulars of this that are at present known are that the flames were first perceived at 9 o'clock this morning. The building, which contained eight or nine rooms, was whollv destroyed, with everything it contained. An unfortunate boy of three or four years of age, the son of Mr. Pope, the owner, was burned to death. It is thought the deceased was the cause of the fire, and that it originated through his playing with matches. The bush-fire at Doctor's Creek was first perceived to-dav at about 10 o'clock, and is supposed to have originated at Campbell's Creek bridge, four or five miles from Stawell. The flames traversed all M'ltellar's ground in the vicinity of Doctor's Creek, and also the farms of Mr. Grinter and Mr. Scallan. The width of the area taken by the ltumes was about 12 miles, the fire appearing to have broken out in four different places simultaneously.
