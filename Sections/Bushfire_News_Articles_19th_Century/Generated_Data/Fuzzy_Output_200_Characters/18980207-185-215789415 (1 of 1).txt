BUSHFIRES AT BARKSTEAD. (FROM OUR CORRESPONDENT.) By the great fires that have been raging In this district during the past week an immense amount of damage has been done. A splendid forest of young timber has been destroyed, and scarcely an acre for many miles escaped. The foliage is shrivelled up, while the thou sands of dead wood logs—the source of most of the mining firewood —are nearly all burnt. The splitters are great sufferers, as all their stacks of firewood have vanished. On Friday night the schoolhouse had a narrow escape, and would have been burnt if the wind had not changed.
