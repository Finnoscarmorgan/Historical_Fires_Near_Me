A New Way to Burn Stubble.— The farmers of the Wimmera seem to have inven«ied a -peculiar method of getting rid of their stubble, preparatory to ploughing the ground for a fresh crop, or, at any rate, the plan, if not new, is one we have never before witnessed. In many of the paddocks along the line of railway, a. pair of horses may be observed dragging behind them what appears to be a portable bush-fire. On closer inspeo- . tion it is seen that the horses are harnessed to an iron harrow clogged with burning straw. The harrow gathers up all the etubble as it goes along, and the straw is consumed as fast as it comes into the harrow.
