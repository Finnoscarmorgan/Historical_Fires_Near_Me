Blackall [FROM OUR OWN CORRESPONDENT.] February 18. FINE rain has fallen at Blackall and as it has the appearance of being a general rain, I trust it may prove to be so. The rain fell through a period of about twelve hours, at times in smart showers, but not as yet sufficiently heavy to make much water. It will, however, give the grass a fine start, which was much wanted, as, from the long continuance of dry weather and the destruction caused in the grass by bush fires, feed was in some places becoming un- pleasantly scarce, although I have not heard of any actual loss.
