BUSH FIRES-Although there is a stringent act In force, and so many have suffered from bush confla grations, still there are people as careless as ever, having no dread of the penalty nor any regard for the property of others. Around Yass bush fires have been raging during the past several days, and we are afraid they will cause a great deal of distress, as the grass had become very scarce. The bush paddock of Mr. F. Larkins at Bango was on fire on Wednesday; but through great exertions the flames were extin guished before reaching the cultivation paddock. A small paddock on the same creek, belonging to Mr. Owen Hilly, also ignited on the same afternoon; but fortunately the greater portion of the grass was saved.
