Camperdown Chronicle. SATURDAY, JANUARY 9, 1886. Our readers will doubtless peruse with interest the account we present to them to-day of the desolation brought about by the bush fires that have been raging in the Heytesbury forest. It would appear from this that in a few hours over a score of families hare been made homeless and almost destitute, through no apparent fault of their own. In one day the savings of years of labor, as represented by the improvements on the various selections, have been des- troyed. The loss, it is admitted on all sides, is a heavy one. The individual losses cannot yet be stated, but it is estimated that thousands of pounds would not cover the damage that has been done.
