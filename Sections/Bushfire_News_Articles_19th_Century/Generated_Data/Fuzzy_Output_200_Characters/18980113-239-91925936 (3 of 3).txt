During the afternoon a south wind set in, bringing the smoke from distant bushfires, but also a cool change the thermometer falling about 20 degrees before .sunset. Rain threatened but only a few drops fell. During the night there was thunder at no great distance. To- day the atmosphere is clear, and the temperature cool and refreshing after the excessive heat. Accounts appear in the Melbourne papers of numerous des tructive bushfires in different parts of the colony. Yesterday three fires sup posed to have been started by a traction, engine, occurred on the Murray Road one nar the school, and two on the Mount Camel Estate, one being at the Lady's Pass and the other where the creek comes close to the road just beyond the homestead. The fires were fortunately quickly got under, only a small quantity of grass being consumed at each place, with some chock and log fencing where the first mentioned fire occurred.
