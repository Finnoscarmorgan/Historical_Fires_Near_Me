The usual pic nic games were indulged in by the chil dren, and everything passed off well. About sundown the children were formed into procession and marched back to town in the same order as they left it in the morning ; but before dispersing for their respective homes three hearty cheers were given for the Sisters of Mercy. With the exception of the prospect of a speedy re-opening of one of the many copper mines in this district, I have nothing to communicate in the mining iu- dusory. Bushfires are making their appearance very early this year, none having taken place in September before the present un- precedent dry season ; at least, so say our oldest residents. Last week fires were seen to spring up in almost every direction, and in some instances home steads were endangered, but were saved by some of the townspeople and police. On last Saturday morning Messrs. E. Bracken and W. Jones made a start for the Upper Murchison goldfields.
