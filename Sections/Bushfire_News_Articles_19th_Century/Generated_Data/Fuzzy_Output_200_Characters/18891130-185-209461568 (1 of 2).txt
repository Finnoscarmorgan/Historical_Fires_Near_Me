OUR AGRICULTURAL LETTER. (FROM OUR SPECIAL REPORTER.) The phenomenal abundance of vegetable growths of every description, coupled to the near approach of the season when heat and drought may be expected, are circumstances indicating the necessity for ex treme carefulness in taking all pos sible precautions against bushlires. In Riverina groups of farmers and graziers here and there are forming bush-fire brigades, to be ready if necessary to ride long dis tances in order to render assistance in case of any outbreak. The propriety of farmers and graziers everywhere adopting some plan of the kind seems self-evident. Crop news from almost everywhere continues fairly favorable. Rust has made its appearance amongst the wheat in some localities; but so far the damage done is not very serious, and for the future we can only hope for the best.
