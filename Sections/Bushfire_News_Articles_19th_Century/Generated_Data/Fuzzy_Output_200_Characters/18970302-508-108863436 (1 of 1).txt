Altogether twenty-one men are employed, and are making good headway consider- ing the material they are working. While the quarry men were at work they uncovered a remarkable looking- hole, which appears to be of considerable depth. It is lined with a crystalised lime formation, evidently another cave. Mr. Wilson, the caves explorer, descended on Saturday with a view of exploring. Twenty odd passengers passed through Hampton by Mr. Cooper's line of coaches on Saturday. Bush- fires are raging in the vicinity of Hampton. The weather is exceedingly dry, rain being badly needed for the potato and corn crops.
