On Friday morning four cases of drunken ness and disorderly conduct were heard before the Police Court, and in each case the persons charged pleaded guilty, and were fined accord ingly. A. Crittenden was charged under the Bushfires Act with lighting a fire in an open place, contrary to the Above Act, whereby valuable property was endangered. He pleaded guilty, and was fined £2 and costs, 10s. The Mary Jane Anderson, the first vessel direct for Port Germein, arrived yesterday from Western Australia, laden with timber for the jetty.
