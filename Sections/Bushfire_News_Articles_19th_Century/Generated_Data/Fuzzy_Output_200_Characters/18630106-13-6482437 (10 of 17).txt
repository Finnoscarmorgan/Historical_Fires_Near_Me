The care- lessness of some travellers, in this instance, is known to have been the cause of the con- flagration, they having left without extin- guishing their camp fires. No great amount of damage was done, but considerable alarm prevailed for some time that the consequences would be most serious.—Daylesford Express, Jan. 5. Dunolly.—Bush fires have been raging for miles round this district during the early part of the week, at the rear of the hospital, and stretching away towards Murphy's-flat. A fearful couflagration was raging on Tuesday on the Cochrane's side of Mount Bealiba, the flames destroying everything in their course. The heat of Wednesday was most intense, and what with the bush fires, a fierce hot wind, and the air filled with dust, it was amongst the most uncomfortable days ever experienced in this township.
