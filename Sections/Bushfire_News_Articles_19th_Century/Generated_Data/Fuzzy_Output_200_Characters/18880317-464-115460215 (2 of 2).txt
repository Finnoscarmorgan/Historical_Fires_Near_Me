Destructive Bush Fiee in the Burrowa Dis trict — On Monday evening an immense volume of smoke was observed north-west of Burro wa. Senior- Sergeant Prior and Mounted-Constable Stephenson hastened to the locality, and were just in time to organize and direct a party to save the homestead of a settler named Grannell. The bush-fire orginated at Gegulaong, and spread with great rapidity for five miles, burning fences, haystacks, and sheds in its career. The settlers turned out, and with numbers from the town, directed by the police, checked the fire about midnight. Were it not for the destruction of fences and haysheds the fire would have been a boon in clearing the vast area of thistles. Rain is badly wanted.
