SERIOUS FIRE AT LOUTH. A WIDOW BURNED OUT. Our correspondent having described the heat and gale on Sunday, says that "nothing could be done throughout the day to check the bush- fires, and the outcome of these must be very disastrous. The most damage would appear to have been done by a fire that started at the Swamp near the village settlement, which, in addition to a great deal of grass, burnt down a house owned by a settler named Dougall McCallum and a tent belonging to another man, which would go to show the rapidity of the flames. In fact they travelled along a almost an incredible pace, and quickly reached Louth Station, where, I really believe, not a single paddock escaped.
