The bush- fires which raged dur- ing the day not for from Melbourne probably contributed to the overpowering heat. Early in the evening, the reflection of a great conflagration was observed in the direction of Geelong; and accounts received in the course of the day show that there was quite a line of bush- fires between that town and Ballarat. Our tele- grams state that the weather in the interior was far more severe than in Melbourne; that bush-fires were in unpleasant proximity to Ballarat, Sandhurst, Daylesford, and other towns; that forests were a-blaze, and that homesteads had been destroyed.
