We were soon once more in the jarrah forest, and the road wound round the rises or over the hills, through what Brunton Stephens aptly calls the 'eucalyptic cloisterdom,' for mile after mile in a most monotonous way, very few clearings being seen and those not of great extent, so that the view was ever shortly bounded by the grey, or where a bush-fire had passed, black - barked boles of the jarrah and redgums. The flowers as usual were very beautiful handsome, glowing-hued Kennedias climbed and trailed over stump and brush, and a brilliant crimson calystemon made the under (growth a blaze of colour, while each little glade was carpeted with golden, rose, and white everlastings in endless profusion. Really, the wild flowers of the West are indescribably lovely.
