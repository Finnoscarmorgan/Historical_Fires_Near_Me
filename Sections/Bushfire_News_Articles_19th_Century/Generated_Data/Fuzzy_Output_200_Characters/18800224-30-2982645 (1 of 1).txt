NOTES FROM YORK. BY PETER SIMPLE. Since my last we have been nearly scorched to death. The heat has been something terrific in its intensity ; large bush-fires raging in every di- rection, while our picturesque little town, en- veloped in smoke, has forcibly reminded one of a beautiful lake on a foggy morning. Happily as yet no serious harm has been done by these de- vastating pests of the summer. The result of our election has put the right man in the right place, and I venture to say given general satisfaction.
