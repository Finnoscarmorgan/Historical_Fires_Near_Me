SOUTH AUSTRALIA, ADELAIDE, Monday Evening. On Wednesday last; Captain Thyle, of the barque Runnymede, lyiug at Pert Pirie, accompanied by four seamen, left the vessel in a boat to communicate with the lightship on the Eastern shoal, respect ing the approaches to Port Pirie. Since then nothing has been heard of them, and as half a gale was blowing at the time they left the Runnymede, it is supposed that the boat has bean swamped before reaching the lightship, and the men drowned. Destructive bush-fires have ociurred at Barossa. Mr. J'jlm Murray, sheep farmer, estimated his loss at 4000.
