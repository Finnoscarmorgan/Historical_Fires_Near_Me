BROUGHTON VALE. The weather continues very dry with occa- sional hot winds, so that considerable anxiety is felt by all classes in consequence of the continued drought. It has been intimated that the different churches should appoint a special service to pray for rain. Not with- standing the mystification of scientific pro- fessurs, there is a current belief in the neces- sity for prayer, and confidence that Almighty God will be entreated and that the calamities incident., to a season of prolonged drought may thus be averted. Experience has proved this in former years, and it is again recommended as expressive of humility and of trust in God. The late bushfires were very threatening and a few have suffered- Mr. A Hanlon and Mr. T. Crawford in particular..
